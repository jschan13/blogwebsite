About The Meridian

We believe good writing still changes minds.

The Meridian is an independent publication for curious people who want more than headlines. We publish long-form essays, reported features, and critical perspectives on the ideas shaping our world.

Our Mission

Founded in 2024, The Meridian started from a simple frustration: the internet has never made it easier to produce words, yet somehow it feels harder than ever to find writing that respects the reader's intelligence and time.

We take a different approach. Every piece we publish is reported, edited, and fact-checked. We don't chase trending topics or optimise for engagement metrics. We write about what we think matters, and we try to do it well.

Our readers span more than 40 countries. They are scientists, teachers, designers, engineers, and students. What they share is an appetite for ideas that don't fit on a bumper sticker.

Editorial Independence

The Meridian is reader-supported. We carry no advertising and accept no sponsored content. Our revenue comes entirely from subscriptions, which means our only obligation is to the people reading this.

We believe this model — unfashionable as it sometimes feels — is the only one that consistently produces writing worth reading. When your reader is your customer, you are accountable to them in the deepest possible sense.

What we stand for

Intellectual honestyWe follow arguments where they lead, not where we'd prefer them to go.
Depth over speedWe'd rather be late and right than first and wrong.
Reader respectWe assume you can handle complexity, nuance, and uncertainty.
IndependenceNo advertisers, no sponsors, no external funders with editorial influence.
AccountabilityWe correct our mistakes prominently and without excuses.
